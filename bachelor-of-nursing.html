About This Program

Everything you need to know about the Bachelor of Nursing at Tarelium Education.

Program Overview

  • The Bachelor of Nursing (BN) is a three-year, full-time program accredited by the Australian Nursing & Midwifery Accreditation Council (ANMAC) and approved by AHPRA, enabling graduates to apply for registration as a Registered Nurse in Australia.
  • The program integrates theory with practice across every year of study. You will complete a minimum of 800 supervised clinical placement hours in hospitals, aged care facilities, community health centres, and mental health services.
  • Teaching is delivered through a combination of interactive lectures, simulation laboratory sessions, small-group tutorials, and clinical skills workshops. Our state-of-the-art nursing simulation suite features high-fidelity manikins and a mock hospital ward.
  • Students develop expertise across medical-surgical nursing, mental health, aged care, paediatrics, critical care, perioperative care, and community nursing — giving you the breadth and depth expected of a confident, practice-ready graduate.
  • Upon completing the degree and meeting AHPRA's English language requirements, graduates may apply for registration as a Registered Nurse (Division 1) in all Australian states and territories.

Why Choose Tarelium?

  • Clinical Partnerships: We have formal clinical placement agreements with over 60 healthcare organisations across NSW, including major metropolitan hospitals, regional health networks, and community services — giving you diverse, real-world experience.
  • Simulation Technology: Our nursing simulation suite is equipped with Laerdal high-fidelity manikins, OSCE assessment stations, and video debriefing technology — among the best-equipped facilities in the sector.
  • Graduate Employment Rate: 95% of our Bachelor of Nursing graduates are employed in a nursing role within 6 months of completing the program (2025 Graduate Outcomes Survey).
  • Small Cohort Sizes: Clinical skills and simulation sessions are capped at 15 students, ensuring personalised feedback and hands-on practice time for every student.
  • Industry-Active Staff: All nursing academics hold current clinical registration and maintain active industry links — you learn from people who practise what they teach.
  • Scholarship Support: Health Sciences students are eligible for the Equity Access Scholarship, Women in STEM Scholarship, and the government-administered Commonwealth-Supported Place (CSP) subsidy for eligible domestic students.

Clinical Placements

Hands-on clinical experience is at the heart of the Bachelor of Nursing. You'll spend time in real healthcare settings from your very first year.

Placement Overview

  • Total hours: A minimum of 800 supervised clinical hours are required to meet ANMAC accreditation standards. Many students complete 900+ hours across their degree.
  • Progression: Placements increase in complexity each year — from foundational observations and basic care tasks in Year 1, to independent clinical management with supervision in Year 3.
  • Scheduling: Placement blocks are scheduled by the Clinical Placement Unit and cannot be deferred unless approved in advance on medical or compassionate grounds. Students must be available for both metropolitan and regional placements.
  • Transition-to-Practice Block: In Year 3 Semester 2, students complete a 10-week consolidated transition block designed to mirror the demands of graduate registered nurse employment — the most significant single placement of the degree.
  • Assessment during placement: Clinical performance is assessed using the ANSAT (Australian Nursing Standards Assessment Tool) at the end of each placement block. A pass/fail outcome is recorded.
  • Fitness to practise: Students must maintain a current Working with Children Check, National Police Check, and all required vaccinations (see Section 7.3 of the Student Clinical Handbook) throughout the degree.

Entry Requirements

What you need to apply for the Bachelor of Nursing for the 2026 intake.

Domestic Students

  • Minimum ATAR: 80.00 (2026 entry). The ATAR cut-off for 2025 was 82.30 — higher ATARs are competitive.
  • Prerequisite subjects: A study score of at least 25 (or equivalent) in one of: Biology, Chemistry, or Health & Human Development. NSW students: Band 4 or higher in a science subject.
  • Non-ATAR pathways: Mature-age applicants (21+) without a qualifying ATAR may apply based on work experience in a health or care setting, supported by a personal statement and a short interview. Applications via UAC or direct to the Admissions Office.
  • TAFE pathway: Holders of the Certificate III or IV in Individual Support (Disability, Aged Care, or Home & Community) from a registered provider may apply with an ATAR adjustment or via direct entry assessment.
  • Second-degree entry: Applicants who hold a bachelor's degree in any discipline and meet the science prerequisite may apply. Selection is based on GPA (minimum 5.0 on a 7-point scale).
  • Pre-enrolment requirements: A current Working with Children Check (Employee), National Police Check (no older than 3 months at commencement), hepatitis B serology, and influenza vaccination are mandatory before clinical placements begin.

International Students

  • Academic: Completion of Year 12 or equivalent secondary education with results equivalent to an Australian ATAR of 80.00. Refer to the International Qualifications guide on the Admissions page for country-specific equivalencies.
  • English language (IELTS Academic): Overall band 7.0, with a minimum of 7.0 in each of the four components (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking). No component below 7.0 is accepted — this is an AHPRA regulatory requirement, not a university discretion.
  • English language (PTE Academic): Overall 65, with no communicative skills score below 65.
  • English language (TOEFL iBT): Overall 94, with minimum component scores: Listening 24, Reading 24, Writing 27, Speaking 23.
  • Visa: A valid Australian student visa (subclass 500) is required. Visa processing time should be factored into your application timeline — we recommend applying at least 4 months before the semester start date.
  • Health insurance: Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC) is mandatory for all international students for the duration of the degree. Students should obtain OSHC before departing for Australia.
  • Pre-enrolment: Same requirements as domestic students apply — Working with Children Check, National Police Check, and vaccination evidence must be provided before clinical placement commences.

How to Apply

Applications for the 2026 intake are open now. Here's everything you need to know about the application and offer process.

Domestic Applicants

  • Via UAC (Universities Admissions Centre): Apply through uac.edu.au. Tarelium Education's UAC institution code is TAR. The Bachelor of Nursing course code is TAR–BN001.
  • Direct applications: Mature-age, second-degree, and TAFE pathway applicants may apply directly to the Tarelium Admissions Office. Download the Direct Application Form from the Admissions page.
  • Preference order: In your UAC application, you may list up to 5 preferences. We recommend listing the Bachelor of Nursing as a high preference to maximise your chance of receiving an early offer.
  • Early offers: Early conditional offers may be made to applicants with a predicted ATAR of 85+ in the September offer round. Early offer recipients must accept within 7 calendar days.
  • Supporting documents: For non-ATAR pathways: personal statement (800 words), evidence of healthcare work or volunteering, and two referee statements from supervisors or educators.

International Applicants

  • Apply directly: Complete the International Student Application Form on the Admissions page and submit with all required documents.
  • Required documents: Certified copies of all academic transcripts; certified English translation (if not in English); current passport; English language test results (IELTS/PTE/TOEFL); personal statement; two academic or professional references.
  • Conditional offer: A conditional offer letter (CoE) will be issued within 10 business days of receiving a complete application. The CoE is required for your student visa application.
  • Agent applications: We accept applications through registered education agents. A list of our authorised agents is available on request from the International Admissions Office.
  • Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE): Your CoE for visa purposes is issued by Student Administration after you have accepted your offer and paid the required enrolment deposit ($5,000 AUD, credited against Semester 1 fees).
